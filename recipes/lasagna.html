Lasagna

Description

This classic lasagna recipe is the ultimate comfort food, featuring layers of tender pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted mozzarella cheese. Each bite offers a perfect balance of savory flavors and satisfying textures, making it a family favorite for weeknight dinners or special occasions.

Made with ground beef simmered in tomato sauce and Italian herbs, this hearty dish is complemented by velvety béchamel sauce that adds a creamy contrast to the tangy tomato base. Topped with a golden, bubbly cheese crust, this lasagna is best served hot with a side of garlic bread and a fresh green salad.

Main Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles (regular or no-boil)
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • Parmesan cheese (grated)
  • Ground beef (can be mixed with ground pork or Italian sausage)
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Olive oil

For the Sauce

  • Canned crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Dried oregano
  • Dried basil
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
  • Sugar (optional, to balance acidity)

Optional Additions

  • Egg (mixed into the ricotta for texture)
  • Fresh parsley or basil (chopped)
  • Spinach (cooked and drained, can be mixed with ricotta)
  • Mushrooms (sautéed)
  • Red wine (adds depth to the meat sauce)

Steps to Make Lasagna

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions (unless using no-boil noodles). Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  4. Add minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds.
  5. Add ground beef (or meat mixture) and cook until browned. Drain excess fat.
  6. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar. Simmer for 15–20 minutes. Add red wine if using.
  7. In a separate bowl, mix ricotta cheese with an egg and chopped parsley or basil (if using).
  8. In a baking dish, start layering: spread a thin layer of sauce on the bottom.
  9. Place a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  10. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture over the noodles.
  11. Sprinkle a layer of mozzarella cheese and a bit of Parmesan.
  12. Repeat layers (sauce, noodles, ricotta, cheeses) until all ingredients are used, ending with sauce and cheese on top.
  13. Cover the dish with aluminum foil (tip: spray the foil with cooking spray to prevent sticking).
  14. Bake for 25 minutes covered, then uncover and bake an additional 20–25 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  15. Let the lasagna rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing and serving.
